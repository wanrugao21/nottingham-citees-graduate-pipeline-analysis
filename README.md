# Nottingham CITEES Graduate Pipeline Analysis

> Multi-source evidence review mapping environmental science graduate outcomes
> across the East Midlands, UK. NERC funded research project.

## Overview

| Item | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Project | NERC CITEES (Connecting, Inspiring, Training and Employing Environmental Scientists) |
| Partners | University of Nottingham, Nottingham Trent University, British Geological Survey, Environment Agency |
| Data sources | DiscoverUni, LinkedIn alumni data, HESA National Graduate Outcomes, NTU internal records |
| Sample size | 7000 plus graduate records |
| Period | December 2025 to May 2026 |

## Key Finding

The East Midlands retains only 5.1 percent of national geography and
environmental graduate employment, ranking 10th of 12 UK regions, despite
environment professionals being the single largest graduate occupation
nationally. This report names this gap the Nottingham Talent Paradox,
arguing it reflects a structural mismatch at the education employer
interface rather than a pipeline volume problem.

## Methods

1. Data integration across four independent sources with documented limitations
2. Regional distribution modelling using HESA CAH26-01 occupational data
3. Time series analysis of graduate destination trends (2022 to 2026)
4. Sankey flow visualisation of programme to sector and regional talent movement
5. Institutional comparison analysis between UoN and NTU graduate profiles
